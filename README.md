# FUTURE_DS_02
Customer Retention &amp; Churn Analysis (Telco Dataset)

 Project Overview

This project analyzes customer churn behavior in a subscription-based telecom business. The goal is to identify why customers leave, which segments are at risk, and what actions can improve retention.

  Objectives
-Measure overall churn and retention rates
-Identify high-risk customer segments
-Analyze customer behavior across contracts, tenure, pricing, and payment methods
-Quantify revenue loss due to churn
-Provide actionable recommendations to improve customer retention

  Key Features:
-tenure – Length of customer relationship
-Contract – Subscription type
-MonthlyCharges – Monthly billing amount
-PaymentMethod – Payment type
-InternetService – Service type
-Churn – Target variable (Yes/No)

  Tools & Technologies
Python (Pandas) – Data cleaning and preprocessing
Power BI – Dashboard creation and visualization
DAX – KPI calculations (churn rate, revenue loss, etc.)

  Key Metrics (KPIs)
Total Customers: ~7,000
Churn Rate: 26.58%
Revenue Loss: 139K (monthly)

  Key Findings
High-risk customers typically:
-Are new users
-Use month-to-month contracts
-Pay higher monthly charges
-Use electronic payment methods

  Business Recommendations
Improve Onboarding
-Provide tutorials and early support
-Offer first-month incentives
Encourage Long-Term Contracts
-Offer discounts for annual plans
-Introduce loyalty rewards
Optimize Pricing Strategy
-Introduce flexible pricing tiers
-Bundle services for better value

The analysis reveals that churn is driven primarily by early-stage customer experience, contract flexibility, pricing, and payment behavior. By addressing these areas, businesses can significantly improve retention and reduce revenue loss
